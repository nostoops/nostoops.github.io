Being from San Diego, I spend most of my free time paddling out or hiking with friends. While I am an Environmental Studies graduate from UC Santa Barbara, I also have a Professional Certificate in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) from UC San Diego. Over the years, my studies and work have primarily gravitated towards environmental planning and compliance using GIS to explore the interactions that bridge spatial and environmental systems.

At Harris and Associates, Inc., I contribute to the GIS workload by preparing custom map figures, web apps, shapefiles, graphics, and tables in support of engineering and environmental planning projects using Esri ArcGIS, related extensions, relational databases, and other software such as Adobe Illustrator and InDesign. I primarily use ArcMap, ArcGIS Pro, and AGO for analyses, data management, and cartography. These tasks include importing and exporting a wide range of data and file types, using a variety of geoprocessing tools, symbolizing features, summarizing GIS calculations, and helping design, create, maintain, and QA/QC geospatial databases using ArcGIS, Python, SQL, Microsoft Excel, and other software.

Various examples of engineering and environmental consulting projects I assist with include project plans, habitat restorations, conservation easements, aquatic resource delineations, mitigated negative declarations, biological resource surveys, and cultural resource surveys, mainly for municipalities or public entities.
